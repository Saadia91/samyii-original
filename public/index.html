Project Cycle

1. Research - The research is probably the most important stage of the cycle. Not only does it involve brainstorming ideas and looking at current trends, it also is an opportunity to refresh ones skills in different areas of web development. This may also involve learning new languages or using different programming libraries. At this stage, you have a fresh canvas and the opportunities seem endless!

2. Design - This is the fun part! The design stage is ongoing as ideas are always changing and things are constantly being added and taken away right up until the very end, in order to get the best result. Once most of the research is done and the project is decided, a draft of the layout is produced. The fonts are then chosen and an Unsplash collection is made with images that could potentially be used in the project.

3. Execution - All projects begin with a Github repository, then, armed with your research, sketches, drafts and Unsplash collections, this is the stage where most things go out of the window! Problems are encountered and (usually) solved, new ideas are developed, tried and tested and the result is a shiny new project that was worth the countless hours you spent overcoming programing issues and design flaws!
